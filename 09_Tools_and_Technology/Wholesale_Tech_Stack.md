# Wholesale Technology Stack

## Overview

Wholesale operations rely on a combination of integrated systems that support order processing, inventory management, logistics coordination, financial transactions, and operational reporting.

These systems form the wholesale technology stack, which enables organizations to manage high volumes of retail partner orders while maintaining operational accuracy and efficiency.

---

## Core Technology Layers

A typical wholesale technology stack includes several integrated system layers.

### Sales and Order Platforms

Sales platforms capture customer orders and provide the interface between retail partners and internal operations.

Examples include:

• NuOrder  
• JOOR  
• retailer portals  
• direct EDI order intake  

These platforms generate purchase orders that enter the operational workflow.

---

### ERP Systems

ERP systems serve as the operational backbone for wholesale organizations.

Common ERP platforms include:

• NetSuite  
• SAP  
• Sage  
• Microsoft Dynamics  

ERP systems manage:

• order processing  
• inventory records  
• financial transactions  
• invoicing and billing

---

### Integration Platforms (EDI)

Integration platforms enable automated communication between retailers and suppliers.

Common EDI providers include:

• SPS Commerce  
• CommerceHub  
• TrueCommerce  

These systems transmit standardized business documents such as:

• purchase orders  
• order acknowledgements  
• advance ship notices  
• invoices

---

### Warehouse and Logistics Systems

Warehouse and logistics systems manage physical product movement.

Examples include:

• Warehouse Management Systems (WMS)  
• Transportation Management Systems (TMS)  
• shipping carrier platforms  

These systems support:

• inventory allocation  
• shipment routing  
• warehouse picking and packing  
• freight booking

---

### Financial Systems

Financial tools ensure accurate billing and payment tracking.

These systems manage:

• invoice generation  
• payment reconciliation  
• chargeback processing  
• financial reporting

Financial workflows are typically integrated within ERP systems.

---

### Analytics and Reporting Tools

Analytics tools provide operational visibility and performance monitoring.

Common tools include:

• Excel  
• Google Sheets  
• Tableau  
• Power BI  
• ERP reporting dashboards

These tools track metrics such as:

• order accuracy  
• fulfillment performance  
• inventory availability  
• chargeback trends

---

## Technology Stack Architecture

A simplified wholesale technology architecture typically looks like this:

Retail Partner Systems  
↓  
Sales / Order Platforms  
↓  
Integration Platforms (EDI / APIs)  
↓  
ERP Systems  
↓  
Warehouse and Logistics Systems  
↓  
Financial Systems  
↓  
Analytics and Reporting Tools

Each layer supports the operational workflow from order receipt through financial reconciliation.

---

## Operational Importance

A well-designed technology stack allows organizations to:

• automate order processing  
• reduce manual data entry  
• improve operational visibility  
• increase order accuracy  
• support scalable growth

Strong system integration ensures reliable data flow between operational teams.

---

## Final Note

Wholesale technology stacks are built to support high-volume order processing and complex retailer relationships. Organizations that maintain strong system integrations and data visibility can operate more efficiently and scale operations with greater confidence.
