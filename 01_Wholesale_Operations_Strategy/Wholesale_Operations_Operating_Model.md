# Wholesale Operations Operating Model

Wholesale operations serve as the execution engine supporting sales organizations that distribute products through retail partners, distributors, or marketplaces.

The operating model for wholesale operations integrates several core capabilities that ensure orders move efficiently from purchase order receipt through shipment and invoicing.

## Core Operational Pillars

Wholesale operations typically operate across five major pillars:

1. Order Management
2. Systems Integration
3. Logistics & Fulfillment
4. Financial Operations
5. Data & Analytics

These pillars are supported by continuous process improvement and operational governance.

## Operational Objectives

The primary objectives of wholesale operations include:

• ensuring order accuracy and compliance  
• coordinating inventory allocation and fulfillment  
• managing system integrations with external partners  
• supporting accurate invoicing and payment reconciliation  
• maintaining operational visibility through analytics

Strong operational governance allows organizations to scale wholesale business efficiently while maintaining retailer confidence.

## Operational Governance

Effective wholesale operations require clear governance across teams including:

• sales operations  
• logistics and fulfillment  
• finance and billing  
• technology and systems integration  
• product and inventory planning

Cross-functional alignment ensures operational issues are identified early and resolved quickly.

Wholesale operations function best when processes are standardized, documented, and continuously improved.
