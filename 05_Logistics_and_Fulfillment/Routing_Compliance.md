# Routing Compliance Framework

## Overview

Routing compliance refers to the process of ensuring that shipments follow the specific logistics and transportation requirements defined by wholesale partners such as retailers, distributors, or marketplaces.

Many partners enforce routing guides that dictate how shipments must be prepared, scheduled, and transported. Failure to follow these guidelines can result in operational disruptions, shipment delays, or financial penalties such as chargebacks.

Wholesale operations teams play a key role in ensuring that shipments meet routing requirements before leaving the warehouse or fulfillment center.

---

# Purpose of Routing Compliance

The routing compliance process ensures that:

• shipments are scheduled according to partner requirements  
• approved carriers and transportation methods are used  
• labeling and documentation standards are met  
• warehouse operations align with shipping timelines  
• shipments arrive at the correct destination without delay  

A well-managed routing process helps maintain strong relationships with wholesale partners while protecting operational efficiency.

---

# Routing Guide Requirements

Retail partners typically publish routing guides that define shipping expectations.

Common routing requirements include:

• approved carriers or freight providers  
• shipment booking procedures  
• delivery appointment scheduling  
• labeling and barcode standards  
• pallet configuration rules  
• carton labeling requirements  

Operations teams must ensure that all shipments follow these requirements before they are released.

---

# Shipment Preparation

Before shipments are scheduled, warehouse teams must prepare the order according to partner requirements.

Preparation steps include:

• verifying order accuracy  
• confirming product quantities  
• packing items according to case pack requirements  
• labeling cartons and pallets  
• preparing shipment documentation  

Proper shipment preparation reduces the risk of compliance violations.

---

# Shipment Scheduling

Once the shipment is prepared, transportation must be scheduled according to routing guide requirements.

Scheduling tasks may include:

• booking freight carriers  
• requesting delivery appointments  
• coordinating pickup schedules with warehouses or 3PL providers  
• confirming transportation timelines

Some retailers require shipments to be booked through specific logistics portals.

---

# Carrier Selection

Routing guides often require organizations to use specific transportation providers.

Carrier selection may depend on:

• shipment size  
• delivery location  
• service level requirements  
• partner carrier agreements  

Using unauthorized carriers may result in compliance penalties.

---

# Shipment Documentation

Each shipment must include proper documentation to ensure successful delivery and receiving.

Common shipment documents include:

• packing lists  
• shipping labels  
• bill of lading (BOL)  
• pallet labels  
• ASN documentation if required

Documentation accuracy is critical for receiving operations at partner facilities.

---

# Shipment Tracking

Operations teams should monitor shipments to ensure that deliveries occur on schedule.

Tracking may include:

• carrier tracking numbers  
• delivery confirmations  
• warehouse dispatch records  
• transportation management system updates

Shipment visibility allows teams to respond quickly to potential delays.

---

# Compliance Monitoring

Organizations should track compliance performance to identify operational risks.

Key compliance metrics include:

• on-time shipment rate  
• routing compliance rate  
• shipment exception rate  
• retailer chargeback frequency

Monitoring these metrics helps identify areas where operational processes can improve.

---

# Common Routing Issues

Several issues commonly occur in wholesale logistics operations.

Examples include:

• incorrect carrier usage  
• shipment scheduling errors  
• labeling violations  
• pallet configuration errors  
• missed delivery appointments

These issues can lead to operational disruptions and financial penalties.

---

# Exception Management

When routing issues occur, operations teams must quickly investigate and resolve the problem.

Resolution may involve:

• coordination with warehouse teams  
• communication with logistics providers  
• documentation of compliance issues  
• corrective action to prevent repeat errors

Strong exception management reduces long-term operational risk.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Routing compliance should be reviewed regularly as organizations onboard new partners or expand distribution channels.

Potential improvements include:

• automation of shipment scheduling  
• better integration with logistics platforms  
• standardized labeling procedures  
• improved warehouse training

Consistent process refinement ensures reliable logistics execution across the wholesale network.
